Aranya Digital Services Ltd — Treasury & FX Summary FY25 (excerpt)

Currency exposure
- ~70% of revenue is USD/GBP-billed (offshore + onsite); a large share of cost is INR (India delivery).
  Net long USD/GBP position creates a natural revenue tailwind when the rupee depreciates.
- Third-party costs that are FX-denominated: onsite contractor staffing (USD/GBP), foreign SaaS
  (ServiceNow, Salesforce, GitHub, Coursera, Udemy, Cisco), and Google Cloud (USD).

Hedge book
- Policy: hedge 50–75% of next-12-month net USD inflows via forwards; layered monthly.
- FY25 average realised USD/INR ~83.2; forward cover extends ~9 months.

Implications for cost analysis
- FX moves can make USD-denominated SaaS and onsite staffing look more expensive in INR YoY even when
  the underlying USD price is flat — separate FX effect from true price/volume before claiming savings.
- Foreign-SaaS rationalisation savings should be modelled in the contract currency, then converted at a
  consistent rate (use ~83.2 USD/INR) for the value bridge.
- Do not attribute FX-driven cost increases to operational inefficiency in benchmarking.
