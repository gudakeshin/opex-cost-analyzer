Deep Research Brief — Indian IT/ITeS cost structure & peer cost-out (for Aranya Digital Services Ltd)

Market context (FY24–FY25)
- Indian IT services growth moderated to mid-single digits as discretionary tech spend tightened in
  BFSI and Retail; clients pushed for vendor consolidation and price concessions.
- Margin pressure across the sector from wage inflation, elevated subcontractor usage during demand
  spikes, and higher cloud pass-through on GenAI workloads.
- Utilisation and pyramid optimisation (fresher mix, bench reduction) are the dominant margin levers;
  large peers target 84–87% billable utilisation.

Peer cost-out programmes (illustrative, from public commentary)
- Tier-1 peers ran multi-quarter subcontractor reduction, cutting subcontractor cost as a % of revenue
  by 150–250 bps by insourcing and rate harmonisation.
- FinOps / cloud cost governance commonly delivers 10–18% of cloud spend via reserved capacity,
  rightsizing, and idle reclaim.
- Software licence rationalisation (shelfware, edition downgrades) typically yields 8–15% of SaaS spend,
  constrained where security/compliance features are bundled.
- Real-estate consolidation under hybrid work delivers 10–20% facilities savings but is gated by SEZ
  lock-ins and lease exit penalties.

Constraints specific to India / Aranya
- SEZ tax benefits tie campus occupancy to continued physical presence — limits aggressive RE exit.
- MSMED Act 45-day payment rule constrains DPO extension on MSME vendors.
- Regulated client workloads cap supplier switching speed (security tooling, data residency).

Implications for benchmarking
- Position addressable third-party OpEx (ex committed/locked spend) for 6–9% net savings.
- Treat people-cost levers (utilisation/pyramid) separately from third-party cost-out to avoid
  double-counting in the value bridge.
