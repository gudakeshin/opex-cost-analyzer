Deep Research Brief — Indian FMCG cost structure (Prakrit Consumer Brands)

Indian FMCG sector revenue growing 8-10% p.a.; margins under pressure from input cost inflation and digital media cost inflation. Advertising-to-sales ratios: Foods 10-14%, Personal Care 12-16%, Home Care 8-12%. Logistics 8-11% of revenue; e-commerce fulfilment premium 2-3x vs traditional.

Peer observations: Agency consolidation (8+ to 3): 10-18% savings on agency fees. Digital media reallocation (TV to programmatic): 8-15% effective CPM reduction. Logistics tender (primary): 8-14% rate reduction with volume consolidation. Contract manufacturing rationalisation: 6-12% on COGS.

Constraints: FSSAI requires approved supplier lists for food ingredients. Brand spend cannot be cut below threshold without market share risk. MSME co-packer payment mandated by MSMED Act.

Addressable marketing and logistics opex ~50-60% of total non-COGS opex.
